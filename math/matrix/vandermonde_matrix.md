## Vandermonde Matrix

#### Definition 1
* $V \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$
* $$\alpha := (\alpha^{1}, \ldots, \alpha^{m})^{\mathrm{T}} \in \mathbb{R}^{m}$$,

$V$ is called a Vandermonde matrix if

$$
    i \in [1:m],
    \
    j \in [1:n],
    \
    V_{j}^{i}
    =
    (\alpha^{i})^{j-1}
    .
$$

In other words, every row is geometric progression of the element of vector $\alpha$.
