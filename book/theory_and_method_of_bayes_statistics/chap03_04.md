## 3.4. Generalization error and emprical error


#### Theorem 3
* $q$
    * is regular for $p(x \mid w)$,

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
    G_{n}
    & := &
        L(w_{0})
        +
        \frac{1}{n}
        \left(
            \frac{
                d
            }{
                2 \beta
            }
            +
            \frac{1}{2}
            \norm{\xi_{n}}^{2}
            -
            \frac{1}{2 \beta}
            \mathrm{tr}
            \left(
                I J^{-1}
            \right)
        \right)
        +
        o_{p}(\frac{1}{n})
    \label{equation_03_14}
    \\
    T_{n}
    & := &
        L_{n}(w_{0})
        +
        \frac{1}{n}
        \left(
            \frac{
                d
            }{
                2 \beta
            }
            -
            \frac{1}{2}
            \norm{\xi_{n}}^{2}
            -
            \frac{1}{2 \beta}
            \mathrm{tr}
            \left(
                I J^{-1}
            \right)
        \right)
        +
        o_{p}(\frac{1}{n})
    \label{equation_03_15}
    \\
    \mathrm{E}
    \left[
        \norm{ \xi_{n}}^{2}
    \right]
    & = &
        \mathrm{tr}
        \left(
            I J^{-1}
        \right)
        +
        o(1)
    \label{equation_03_16}
\end{eqnarray}
$$
