## 3.4 Newton's Method With Hessian Modification


#### Definition inertia
* $A \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}$,
    * symmetric matrix
* $\lambda_{i}(A)$,
    * $i$-th leargest eigenvalues

Let

$$
\begin{eqnarray}
    n_{+}(A)
    & := &
        \mathrm{card}(
            \{
                i = 1, \ldots, n
                \mid
                \lambda_{i}(A)
                >
                0
            \} 
        )
    \nonumber
    \\
    n_{-}(A)
    & := &
        \mathrm{card}(
            \{
                i = 1, \ldots, n
                \mid
                \lambda_{i}(A)
                <
                0
            \} 
        )
    \nonumber
    \\
    n_{0}(A)
    & := &
        \mathrm{card}(
            \{
                i = 1, \ldots, n
                \mid
                \lambda_{i}(A)
                =
                0
            \} 
        )
    \nonumber
\end{eqnarray}
$$

Inertia of a matrix $A$ is a triplet $$\mathrm{inertia}(A) := (n_{+}, n_{-}, n_{0})$$.
